About Me

Hi, I’m Anisa, I use they/them pronouns, and I’m a second year Computational Media major at Georgia Tech! I often say I’m an art major at a tech school as all the majors I’ve explored are influenced by my love of design. At the core of it all:

Design Journey

Starting college I wasn’t completely confident about what I wanted to do in the grand scheme of things. The only thing I did know was that whatever it was needed to involve design, and thus I entered the College of Design as an architecture major.


After realizing architecture wasn't the right fit for me, I switched to computational media, the intersection where computer science meets design.

Portfolio

Objective: "Design a brand/corporation identity system using examples of professionally designed style guides as inspiration. This style guide should reflect your brand design work in a professional matter"

Teammate: Zarya Ajasin
Software: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe InDesign, Figma
I took many roles in this project. First and foremost, I have a drive to make things more accesible, so I played an integral part in coming up with the brand's purpose. I had also been thinking about reimagining an accesibility symbol as I don't feel the official one is representative of the wide range of disabilities people can have. The key iconography within the symbol comes from my idea that the world is full of doors that are automatically closed to some people, and that with a key they can open those doors.

While this was a collaborative effort for all of the elements my main focuses were the logo iterations, the business card, the website, and the merchandise for the company.
